## Learning objective

Know the names of your classmates.

## Key ideas

- Algorithms are all around us
- Choosing algorithms matter
- Learning requires thinking about things and practicing recalling them

## Guided practice

Round 1: Form groups of four. Introduce yourselves and share one fun fact each about yourself or about something you did this summer. When I tell you, you'll form a new group. However, you must be in a group with all new people. Figure it out. We'll repeat until everyone has met (or—more likely—until things break down.)

Round 2: Speed introductions. Move the island tables out of the way and line up in two lines in the center of the room facing each other. Your partner is the person across from each other. Introduce yourself and share a fun fact. When I tell you, rotate one position. (Except the anchor person.) (10 seconds per rotation.) Try to remember as many names as you can.

Round 3: Spaced repetition. Pick a random person. In each pair, say the other person's name if you know it. Get corrected if needed. When I say, each pair should join up with a nearby pair. First, everybody takes a turn saying the name of one of the people in the group, starting with the people whose names you're most sure of. Pay attention to the new people's names so you can name them when it's your turn. Try to say each person's name even if you just learned it. The person being named will correct anyone who gets it wrong or mispronounces their name. Keep going until everyone has named everyone else in the group. When I say, fours join to make eights. Same procedure for reviewing. If we have time start over with new groups of two which combine, etc.

Round 4: Gossip protocol. Pick a random person. Make sure you know each other's names. Then spend a minute naming as many of the other students in the room as you can. Try to learn any names your partner knew that you didn't. When I tell you, pick a new random partner and repeat. (30-60 seconds per rotation).

## Independent practice

Round 1: Look around the room and on your whiteboard, write down the names of each person whose name you know. After a minute, you will have a chance to compare notes with your neighbors and see if you can fill in any blanks any of you have.

Round 2: wipe your whiteboard and do it again.
